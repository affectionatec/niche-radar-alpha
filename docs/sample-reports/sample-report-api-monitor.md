## Go/No-Go Rationale (A6)

**Verdict: 🔴 NO-GO**

Despite genuine user pain around monitoring complexity and cost, this opportunity fails the competition gap test. At least 6 well-funded competitors already serve this exact niche, including free options (UptimeRobot, Uptime Kuma). The barrier to switching is low, and there's no defensible moat for a new entrant. The trend momentum is flat — this is a solved problem being served by mature products.

**Top reason not to:** Market is saturated with free and cheap alternatives. No clear differentiation angle.

**Killer risk:** UptimeRobot offers a free tier that covers 90% of indie developer needs. Competing on price against free is not viable.

**Conditions to reconsider:** If you identified a specific underserved sub-niche (e.g., monitoring for specific frameworks, or API performance regression detection tied to deployments), there might be a narrow angle. But generic uptime monitoring is a dead end.
